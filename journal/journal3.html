The article, "10 Intriguing Photographs to Teach Close Reading and Visual Thinking Skills", encourages the reader to stop and take a more careful look at photos. The website The Unconventional Gallery, by makemepulse, has a similar effect. It caught my eye because the interactive website allows you to scroll through the gallery of projects and works, as if you were browsing through one. I thought the scrolling connected with the walking was a unique element because it creates a unique environment and theme for the website. Most often, individuals display their works with a vertical scroll. However, the background taking place in a gallery makes the viewer slow down and want to engage with the content presented.
